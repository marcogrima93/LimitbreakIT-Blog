It's the moment when foldables stop being a gimmick and become a genuine alternative to the traditional phone-plus-tablet setup that's dominated consumer tech for a decade.

The durability improvements are real too. Samsung's made the hinge mechanisms tighter and more reliable. Huawei showed a tri-fold prototype earlier this year, but Samsung's gotten there first with a production-ready device. That matters. First-mover advantage in foldables is huge because the supply chain, parts manufacturing, and ecosystem integration take time to scale.

The real question: **Can Samsung manufacture enough units?** Triple-fold screens are exponentially more complex than double-fold designs. Panel yields are going to be tighter. Initial availability will probably be limited, which will drive hype even higher.
